# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Kern is a technical project with a clear philosophy: understanding over delegation, reasoning over shortcuts.

We expect the same standard from everyone who participates in this community, whether contributing code, opening issues, writing documentation, or engaging in discussions.

Be direct. Be respectful. Be useful.

## Expected behaviour

- Communicate clearly and constructively
- Respect different levels of experience
- Focus on the technical problem, not the person
- Accept feedback without defensiveness
- Give feedback without cruelty

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Dismissing contributions without explanation
- Gatekeeping based on experience level, nationality, or background
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project

## Enforcement

If you witness or experience behaviour that violates this code, open an issue marked `conduct` or contact the maintainer directly via GitHub.

Violations will be reviewed and may result in removal from the project.
